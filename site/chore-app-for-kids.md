## What to look for in a chore app (so it actually gets used)

A good chore app should make life easier, not add more "admin work." These are the features that matter most:

- <strong>Parent approvals</strong> (chores only count when done)
- <strong>A short daily routine</strong> (so it becomes a habit)
- <strong>Points/coins</strong> (simple values kids understand)
- <strong>Rewards</strong> (screen time, privileges, experiences)
- <strong>Progress kids can see</strong> (streaks, levels, or visible goals)

If the system is too complicated, it won’t last.

## A simple chore app setup (10 minutes)

If you want the fastest setup that works, follow this:

<ol class="article-steps">
  <li><strong>Pick 3–5 chores total.</strong> Start with 1–2 daily chores and 1 weekly chore.</li>
  <li><strong>Assign simple coin values.</strong> Example: 1 coin (quick), 2 coins (standard), 4 coins (weekly bigger job).</li>
  <li><strong>Choose 3–5 rewards.</strong> Keep the reward menu small to avoid constant negotiation.</li>
</ol>

For a step-by-step walkthrough, see <a href="/how-to-set-up-a-chore-chart-for-kids/">how to set up a chore chart for kids</a>.

## FAQ

<ul class="faq-stack">
  <li class="faq-card">
    <h3>What is the best chore app for kids?</h3>
    <p>The best chore app is the one your family will actually use consistently. Look for a short daily routine, parent approvals, and earned rewards like screen time. Simplicity matters more than endless features.</p>
  </li>

  <li class="faq-card">
    <h3>How do chore apps work?</h3>
    <p>Most chore apps let parents assign chores, kids complete them, and parents approve results. Many apps add points/coins so kids can redeem rewards and track progress.</p>
  </li>

  <li class="faq-card">
    <h3>Should kids get paid for chores?</h3>
    <p>You don’t have to pay cash for every chore. Many families keep baseline responsibilities expected and use coins/points for extra effort — redeemed for rewards like earned screen time or a small weekly payout.</p>
  </li>

  <li class="faq-card">
    <h3>How do you motivate a child to do chores?</h3>
    <p>Motivation improves when chores are clear, small, repeatable, and connected to visible progress. Earned rewards work best when they’re capped and consistent instead of negotiated daily.</p>
  </li>

  <li class="faq-card">
    <h3>Is screen time a good reward?</h3>
    <p>It can be, especially when it’s earned in small blocks with clear limits. Many families find earned screen time reduces arguments because kids have a clear path to "yes."</p>
